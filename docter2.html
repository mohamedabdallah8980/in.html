Dr. Magdy Yacoub:Savior of hearts

Heart surgery icon

about of the doctor

Magdy Habib Yacoub (born November 16, 1935) is an Egyptian professor and prominent heart surgeon. He was born in the city of Belbeis, Sharkia Governorate, Egypt, to a Coptic Orthodox family, whose origins are from Minya.

Dr. Magdy Yaqoub is an international Egyptian heart surgeon, best known for his pioneering contributions in the field of heart surgery, especially heart transplantation. He is considered one of the most important figures in the field of medicine in the world.

Dr. Yaqoub is considered a role model for the new generation of doctors, and he has received many international awards and honors in recognition of his contributions to the field of medicine.

Educational stage

He studied medicine at Cairo University, studied in Chicago, then moved to Britain in 1962 to work at the Chest Hospital in London. He then became a specialist in heart and lung surgery at Harefield Hospital (from 1969 to 2001), and director of the Scientific Research and Education Department (since 1992). He was appointed a professor at the National Heart and Lung Institute in 1986, and has been interested in developing heart transplant surgical techniques since 1967.
